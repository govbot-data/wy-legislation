1 1-44-103. False claims liability; damages; costs;
2 civil penalties; exceptions.
3
4 (a) Notwithstanding any other law to the contrary,
5 any person who commits any of the following acts shall be
6 liable to the state for three (3) times the amount of
7 damages that the state sustains because of the actions of
8 that person. A person who commits any of the following acts
9 shall also be liable to the state for the costs of a civil
10 action brought to recover penalties or damages and shall be
11 liable to the state for a civil penalty of not less than
12 five thousand five hundred dollars ($5,500.00) and not more
13 than eleven thousand dollars ($11,000.00) for each false or
14 fraudulent claim where the person:

16 (i) Knowingly presents, or causes to be
17 presented, a false or fraudulent claim for payment or
18 approval;
19
20 (ii) Knowingly makes, uses or causes to be made
21 or used a false record or statement material to a false or
22 fraudulent claim;

14 (a) A civil action brought under this act shall not

16 the facts material to the right of action are known or
17 reasonably should have been known by the attorney general
18 or other prosecuting authority, but in no event more than
19 six (6) years after the date where the violation of this
20 act was committed.

22 (b) In any civil action brought under this act, the
23 state shall be required to prove all essential elements of

7 plea of guilty or nolo contendere, shall estop the
