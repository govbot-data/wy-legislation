4 9-20-201. Legislative findings.
5
6 (a) The legislature finds that:
7
8 (i) Hostile legislative mandates in other
9 jurisdictions constitutes an unconstitutional burden on
10 Wyoming's interstate commerce;
11
12 (ii) Wyoming possesses unique mineral wealth
13 that is subject to regional price suppression due to the
14 actions of other jurisdictions;
15
16 (iii) By converting natural gas into value-added
17 manufactured products in Wyoming, Wyoming can assert its
18 right to engage in global trade by the distribution of
19 products that can no longer be defined as fossil fuel
20 feedstocks.
21

20 the area of the industrial sovereign zone which may include
21 all or any portion of the county or surrounding counties as

8 9-20-204. Gold standard certification.
9
10 (a) The authority shall establish standards of
11 certification for any value-added manufactured product that
12 is produced in Wyoming to promote the special attributes of
13 the manufactured product. The authority shall establish a
14 certification for any manufactured product that certifies
15 that the product is a gold standard Wyoming product if the
16 producer of the manufactured product demonstrates that the
17 product is produced with not more than two-tenths percent
18 (0.2%) methane intensity.
19
20 (b) The use of any certification standards
21 established under subsection (a) of this section by any
22 producer of a manufactured product shall be on a voluntary
23 basis. The authority may collect fees on an annual basis,
