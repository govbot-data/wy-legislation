2 16-4-701. Definitions.
3
4 (a) As used in this article:
5
6 (i) "Government entity" means any municipal,
7 county or state government in Wyoming and any agency,
8 department or entity thereof;
9
10 (ii) "Public funds" means any money, funds,
11 credit, property, tax benefit, subsidy, grant, loan,
12 deferral or infrastructure support provided by a government
13 entity;
14
15 (iii) "Public-funded project" means any
16 proposal, negotiation, agreement or activity that uses or
17 commits public funds or that creates any ongoing
18 obligation, financial liability, land-use impact,
19 infrastructure demand or utility load on a Wyoming city,
20 town or county or a government entity;
21
22 (iv) "Public official" means any officer,
23 employee, agent or servant of a government entity,

5 16-4-702. Public officials; certain nondisclosure
6 agreements prohibited; penalties; remedies.
7
8 (a) No public official shall enter into any
9 nondisclosure agreement or any other agreement that
10 prohibits, prevents or precludes the public official from
11 disclosing any information related to any one (1) or more
12 of the following:
13
14 (i) The use or commitment of public funds;
15
16 (ii) Any public obligations, incentives or
17 financial liabilities to be disbursed, awarded or incurred
18 under the agreement;
19
20 (iii) Impacts on land use, valuation,
21 infrastructure, water or power;
22
3 HB0135

2026 STATE OF WYOMING 26LSO-0482
1 (iv) Information otherwise subject to disclosure
2 under the Public Records Act or information that is
3 required to be disclosed or provided in an open meeting
4 under W.S. 16-4-401 through 16-4-408.
5
6 (b) Any agreement entered into in violation of
7 subsection (a) of this section is void and unenforceable as
8 contrary to public policy.
9
10 (c) No public official shall negotiate or pursue any
11 public-funded project if another party requires
12 nondisclosure as a condition of discussion or due
13 diligence.
14
15 (d) Nothing in this article shall be construed to
16 prohibit or prevent a public official from protecting or
17 limiting information from disclosure only to the extent
18 permitted under W.S. 16-4-203(d).
19
20 (e) Any public official who violates this section is
21 guilty of a misdemeanor punishable upon conviction by a
22 fine of not more than one thousand dollars ($1,000.00).
23

1 (f) Violation of this section constitutes sufficient
